---
title: "Multi-step Forms"
description: "When and how to use the multi-step form layout for higher conversion."
---

DonorPress forms support two layouts: **single-step** (everything on one screen) and **multi-step** (broken across screens). Multi-step usually outperforms single-step on longer forms.

## When to use multi-step

Use multi-step when your form has:

- More than 4–5 donor fields beyond name and email (phone, company, address, etc.)
- A custom comment / "tribute" field
- Complex amount choices (e.g. tied to specific giving levels)
- Anonymity, gift-aid, or other compliance opt-ins

Use single-step when your form is minimal: amount + name + email + payment. Adding multi-step to a tiny form just adds friction.

## Default step structure

When you switch a form to multi-step, the builder splits your fields across three steps:

1. **Amount** — preset amounts, custom amount, recurring toggle.
2. **Details** — donor fields (name, email, address, etc.).
3. **Payment** — gateway selection and payment input.

You can rearrange fields between steps, rename steps, and add/remove steps in the builder.

## Adding or removing steps

In the builder's right-hand layout panel:

- **Add a step** — click **+ Step** at the bottom of the step list.
- **Rename a step** — click the step title and type.
- **Reorder** — drag steps up/down.
- **Delete** — click the trash icon. Fields in the deleted step move to the previous one (no data loss).

## Step navigation

The donor moves through steps using:

- A **Continue** button on each step (disabled until required fields are filled).
- A **Back** button on every step except the first.
- A progress indicator at the top (dots or numbered steps — configurable).

## Validation

Each step validates its fields before allowing the donor to continue. Server-side validation runs on the final submit.

## Saving partial submissions

If a donor abandons a multi-step form mid-flow, no donation is created. The partial state is **not** persisted server-side — it lives only in the browser session.

If you want to track abandonment, look at the `donorpress/form/step_completed` JavaScript event in your analytics integration.

## Tips for higher conversion

<AccordionGroup>
	<Accordion title="Put recurring on step 1">
		Donors who're going to commit to recurring decide early. Showing the option on step 1 (rather than step 3) consistently raises recurring opt-in.
	</Accordion>
	<Accordion title="Keep step 1 to 2-3 fields max">
		The first step is the highest-friction step. The fewer fields, the higher the chance the donor reaches step 2.
	</Accordion>
	<Accordion title="Show the amount on every step">
		A small "$50 monthly" reminder in the step header reduces the number of donors who back out at payment.
	</Accordion>
	<Accordion title="Use a progress indicator">
		Donors who can see "Step 2 of 3" are more likely to complete than donors who feel they're in a bottomless form.
	</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Switching between layouts

You can switch between single-step and multi-step at any time without losing any field configuration. Switching from multi-step to single-step flattens all steps into one. Switching back re-applies the default 3-step split.
