A **donor** is a person (identified by email) who has made at least one donation through DonorPress. Donor records are created automatically — you don't need to import them upfront.

## How donors get created

Every time a donation form is submitted, DonorPress:

1. Looks up the donor by email.
2. If found, adds the donation to that donor and recalculates lifetime totals.
3. If not found, creates a new donor record from the form data.

This means **email is the unique key** for donors. Two donations with the same email always belong to the same donor, regardless of name spelling or capitalization.

## Donor list columns

| Column | Description |
| --- | --- |
| **Name** | First + last name. |
| **Email** | Primary email. |
| **Donations** | Lifetime count of completed donations. |
| **Total** | Lifetime sum of completed donations. |
| **Largest** | Largest single donation. |
| **First donation** | Date of their first completed donation. |
| **Last donation** | Date of their most recent donation. |

Use the search box to find donors by name or email.

## Donor detail screen

Click any donor to open their record. You'll see:

- **Profile card** — name, email, address (if collected), date created.
- **Stats** — total donated, donation count, largest donation, average.
- **Donations tab** — full list of every donation (one-time + recurring renewals).
- **Subscriptions tab** — active and historical recurring plans.
- **Notes tab** — internal team notes.
- **Activity tab** — every event involving this donor.

From here you can:

- Edit name, email, phone, company, address.
- Mark the donor as **anonymous** (hides their name on public-facing campaign lists).
- Manually link the donor to a WordPress user account.
- Recalculate stats (rarely needed — happens automatically).
- Delete the donor (preserves their donation records but unlinks them).

## Linking donors to WordPress users

Donors and WordPress users are separate concepts in DonorPress:

- A **donor** is anyone who's given money. They don't need a WP account.
- A **WordPress user** is anyone who can log into WP. They might or might not have donated.

When a logged-in user makes a donation, DonorPress tries to match by email. You can also manually link them from the donor detail screen.

This matters for the [donor dashboard](/donors/donor-dashboard): a donor can only access their dashboard if they're logged into a WP user account whose email matches their donor record.

## Anonymous donations

If a donor checks **"Make this donation anonymous"** on a form:

- The donor record is still created (you need it for the receipt).
- Their name is hidden on public-facing campaign donor lists.
- Their name is **still visible** in the admin — anonymity is a public-facing setting only.

You can flip a donor's anonymity setting at any time from their detail screen.

## Merging duplicate donors

Occasionally a donor uses two different email addresses (e.g. `sam@gmail.com` and `sam@work.com`). To merge:

<Steps>
	<Step title="Open the duplicate donor">
		Pick the one with fewer donations — that one will be removed.
	</Step>
	<Step title='Click "Merge into another donor"'>
		Search for the donor you want to keep.
	</Step>
	<Step title="Confirm">
		All donations from the duplicate move to the canonical donor. Their email becomes a secondary email on the canonical record. Stats are recalculated.
	</Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
	Merge is irreversible. There's a confirmation step but no undo.
</Warning>

## Exporting donors

From the donors list, **Export** generates a CSV with every donor field plus their lifetime totals. Useful for CRM imports and end-of-year reports.

## Bulk actions

- **Send email** — open a compose window prefilled with the selected donors as recipients (uses your WordPress mailer).
- **Export selected** — CSV of just the selected rows.
- **Delete** — permanently removes donor records. Donations remain but are unlinked.
