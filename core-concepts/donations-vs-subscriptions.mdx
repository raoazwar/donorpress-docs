---
title: "Donations vs. Subscriptions"
description: "Understand the difference between one-time donations and recurring subscriptions in DonorPress, and which model fits each fundraising scenario."
---

People new to DonorPress (especially those coming from WooCommerce or Easy Digital Downloads) often ask: *"Why are donations and subscriptions separate things?"*

Here's the model.

## Donation = one charge

A **donation** is a single payment. One row in `wp_donorpress_donations`. It has:

- An amount
- A status (Complete, Refunded, etc.)
- A donor
- A gateway transaction ID

Every payment that ever leaves a donor's bank account is a donation, full stop.

## Subscription = the recurring plan

A **subscription** is the *agreement* to pay on a recurring schedule. One row in `wp_donorpress_subscriptions`. It has:

- An amount per renewal
- A frequency (monthly, yearly, etc.)
- A status (Active, Paused, Cancelled, etc.)
- A donor
- A gateway subscription ID (Stripe Subscription, PayPal Billing Agreement, etc.)

A subscription **does not move money on its own.** It produces donations.

## How they connect

```
Donor: Sam
Subscription #42 (Active, $10/month)
  └─ Donation #501 (Complete, $10) — first charge
  └─ Donation #517 (Complete, $10) — month 2 renewal
  └─ Donation #533 (Complete, $10) — month 3 renewal
  └─ Donation #549 (Failed,  $10) — month 4 — gateway declined
  └─ Donation #565 (Complete, $10) — month 5 (gateway retried)
```

One subscription, five donations. Each renewal creates a fresh donation record.

## Why separate?

Three reasons:

1. **Donations are the money.** Reports, totals, receipts, exports all live at the donation level. If donations and subscriptions were one table, reporting would constantly need conditional joins.
2. **Status lifecycles are different.** A donation moves through Complete → Refunded. A subscription moves through Active → Paused → Cancelled. Mashing them together makes the state machine confusing.
3. **One-time donations have no subscription.** ~70% of donations on most sites are one-time. They'd carry a permanently-null `subscription_id` if everything were one table.

## Where this comes up

| Action | Where it happens |
| --- | --- |
| Refund a single charge | Donations admin |
| Cancel future renewals | Subscriptions admin |
| See total raised this year | Donations report |
| See active recurring revenue | Subscriptions report (MRR) |
| Email a receipt | Donation event (every renewal triggers a fresh receipt) |
| Email a "subscription cancelled" notice | Subscription event |

## Quick mental model

> "Subscriptions schedule donations. Donations move money."

If you keep that one sentence in mind, the rest of the data model falls into place.
