DonorPress integrates with **PayPal Commerce Platform** (the modern PayPal API). It supports both PayPal balance payments and card payments processed through PayPal.

## Before you start

You need:

- A [PayPal Business account](https://www.paypal.com/business) (personal accounts can't process payments).
- Access to the [PayPal Developer dashboard](https://developer.paypal.com/) where you'll create REST API credentials.
- HTTPS on your WordPress site.

## Step 1 — Create a REST API app

In the PayPal Developer dashboard:

<Steps>
	<Step title="Open My Apps & Credentials">
		Visit [https://developer.paypal.com/dashboard/applications](https://developer.paypal.com/dashboard/applications).
	</Step>
	<Step title="Create an app">
		Click **Create App**.
		- **App Name**: e.g. "DonorPress Production"
		- **App Type**: Merchant
	</Step>
	<Step title="Copy credentials">
		The new app screen shows:
		- **Client ID**
		- **Secret** (click "Show")

		PayPal gives you separate sandbox + live credentials. Use sandbox first.
	</Step>
</Steps>

## Step 2 — Paste them into DonorPress

1. Go to **DonorPress → Settings → Payment Gateways**.
2. Toggle **PayPal** on.
3. Paste sandbox credentials into the **Test** fields.
4. Paste live credentials into the **Live** fields when ready.
5. Save.

## Step 3 — Configure the webhook

<Steps>
	<Step title="Open PayPal webhooks">
		PayPal Developer dashboard → your app → **Add Webhook**.
	</Step>
	<Step title="Set the webhook URL">
		```text
		https://yoursite.com/wp-json/donorpress/v2/gateways/paypal/webhook
		```
	</Step>
	<Step title="Select events">
		Choose:
		- `CHECKOUT.ORDER.APPROVED`
		- `CHECKOUT.ORDER.COMPLETED`
		- `PAYMENT.CAPTURE.COMPLETED`
		- `PAYMENT.CAPTURE.REFUNDED`
		- `PAYMENT.CAPTURE.DENIED`
		- `BILLING.SUBSCRIPTION.ACTIVATED`
		- `BILLING.SUBSCRIPTION.CANCELLED`
		- `BILLING.SUBSCRIPTION.SUSPENDED`
		- `BILLING.SUBSCRIPTION.PAYMENT.FAILED`
	</Step>
	<Step title="Copy the webhook ID">
		After saving, copy the **Webhook ID** (a UUID-like string).
	</Step>
	<Step title="Paste it into DonorPress">
		**Settings → Payment Gateways → PayPal → Webhook ID**.
	</Step>
</Steps>

## Step 4 — Test a donation

In sandbox mode, use [PayPal sandbox test accounts](https://developer.paypal.com/dashboard/accounts):

- A **personal** sandbox account (the donor)
- A **business** sandbox account (your charity — auto-created)

Make a small donation, log into the personal sandbox account in the PayPal popup, approve the payment. The donation should appear in DonorPress with status **Complete**.

## Donor flow

1. Donor clicks "Donate with PayPal" on your form.
2. A PayPal popup (or full-page redirect on mobile) opens.
3. Donor logs into PayPal or enters card details.
4. PayPal returns the donor to your site.
5. DonorPress captures the order and shows the receipt page.

## Subscriptions on PayPal

PayPal recurring donations use **PayPal Subscriptions**. DonorPress creates a Plan and a Subscription on the PayPal side; PayPal handles the renewal cadence and notifies DonorPress via webhooks.

A few PayPal-specific quirks:

- **Frequency limits**: PayPal supports daily, weekly, monthly, every-3-months, every-6-months, and yearly. Custom intervals (e.g. every 5 days) aren't supported.
- **Updating amount**: You can't change the amount of an existing PayPal subscription. The donor has to cancel and create a new one.
- **Currency lock**: PayPal subscriptions are locked to the currency they were created in.

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
	<Accordion title='"Authentication failed" or 401 errors'>
		The Client ID and Secret must come from the same app. Re-copy both from PayPal and paste freshly.
	</Accordion>
	<Accordion title="Webhook ID mismatch">
		PayPal's webhook signature verification compares against the Webhook ID you've stored. If you regenerate the webhook in PayPal, you must also update the ID in DonorPress.
	</Accordion>
	<Accordion title="Sandbox works but live fails">
		Confirm your live PayPal Business account is fully approved (no pending verification steps), and that you've pasted live credentials into the live fields — not the sandbox ones.
	</Accordion>
	<Accordion title='Donor sees "Sorry, we cannot process this transaction"'>
		Usually means the donor's PayPal account or card was declined. The PayPal modal will sometimes give them more detail; check your PayPal transaction log for the specific reason.
	</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
