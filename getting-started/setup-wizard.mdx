After activating DonorPress for the first time, the setup wizard launches automatically. It walks you through the essentials so you can take your first donation without hunting through settings.

<Tip>
	You can revisit the wizard any time from **DonorPress → Tools → Setup Wizard**.
</Tip>

## Welcome screen

The very first screen of the wizard is a celebratory welcome — a dark mesh-gradient hero with brand-color accents, a brief confetti burst, and three glass-style stat pills:

- **Database ready** — confirms tables were created on activation.
- **60-second setup** — sets expectations for the rest of the flow.
- **Secure by default** — reminder that no real charges happen in test mode.

The confetti animation respects `prefers-reduced-motion` and is automatically suppressed for users who have enabled that accessibility setting. The screen only fires on the very first activation; after the wizard has been completed (or skipped) once, the redirect is never triggered again.

## What the wizard configures

<Steps>
	<Step title="Organization details">
		Your charity / business name, country, and the email address that will appear on donation receipts.
	</Step>
	<Step title="Currency">
		Your default currency. Donations are stored in this currency unless the [Currency Switcher module](/modules/currency-switcher) is active.
	</Step>
	<Step title="First payment gateway">
		Pick Stripe or PayPal and paste your test API keys. You can add more gateways later from **Settings → Payment Gateways**.
	</Step>
	<Step title="Receipt page">
		The wizard creates a "Donation Confirmation" page automatically. Donors are redirected here after a successful payment.
	</Step>
	<Step title="First donation form">
		The wizard scaffolds a starter form with three preset amounts so you have something live in under five minutes.
	</Step>
</Steps>

## Skipping or revisiting steps

Each step has a **Skip for now** link in the lower-right. Skipped steps remain accessible from the main settings later — nothing is locked.

If you skip the gateway step, the resulting form will still render, but submitting it shows a friendly "no payment methods enabled" message instead of charging anything.

## What the wizard does *not* do

- It doesn't configure email notifications. See [Email Notifications](/emails/overview) for that.
- It doesn't enable any premium modules. Visit **Settings → Modules** to opt in.
- It doesn't switch DonorPress to live mode. You stay in test mode until you explicitly flip the switch.
