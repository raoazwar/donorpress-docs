The Donor report focuses on the people, not the money — useful for fundraising teams thinking about acquisition, retention, and stewardship.

## Headline numbers

| Metric | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Total donors | Unique donors who've ever given. |
| New donors | First-time donors in the selected period. |
| Returning donors | Donors who've given before, gave again in the period. |
| Active recurring | Donors with at least one Active subscription. |
| Average lifetime value | Total raised / total donor count. |
| Retention rate | % of donors who gave in both this period and the previous period. |

## Acquisition chart

Time-series of new donors per period. Use this to track:

- Whether a campaign brought in net-new donors (vs. existing donors giving more).
- Seasonal patterns (year-end giving, quiet summer, etc.).
- Long-term acquisition health.

## Returning vs. new

A stacked bar chart showing the split between new donors and returning donors per period. Key insight: a healthy charity sees ~60-70% returning donors over time. Lower numbers mean weak retention; higher means weak acquisition.

## Top donors

Table of the highest lifetime-value donors, with:

- Name + email.
- Lifetime total.
- Donation count.
- Last donation date.
- Recurring status.

Useful for major-gift cultivation lists.

## Lapsed donors

Donors who haven't given in the last 365 days. Filter by:

- Lifetime value (focus on high-LTV lapsed donors).
- Last donation date (recent lapses are easier to recover).
- Gateway, form, campaign.

Export this list and run a re-engagement campaign — winning back a lapsed donor is typically 5x cheaper than acquiring a new one.

## LTV histogram (Advanced Dashboard)

If the [Advanced Dashboard](/modules/advanced-dashboard) is active, you also get a histogram showing the distribution of donor lifetime values:

- $1-25
- $26-100
- $101-500
- $501-2,500
- $2,501-10,000
- $10,001+

Most charities follow a power-law distribution (a few high-LTV donors and a long tail of small ones). Spotting a thin top tier suggests an opportunity for major-gift fundraising.

## Filters

- **Date range** — for new / returning / retention metrics.
- **First-time vs. all** — toggle to focus on first-time donor analysis.
- **Currency** — filter to one currency for multi-currency sites.

## Exporting

CSV export includes the full donor list (within the period filter) with lifetime totals and last donation date.

## Stewardship use cases

<AccordionGroup>
	<Accordion title="Thank top donors personally">
		Filter by lifetime value, sort by descending. Call or email the top 20 quarterly.
	</Accordion>
	<Accordion title="Re-engage lapsed donors">
		Lapsed donor list → personalised "we miss you" email with the donor's lifetime impact.
	</Accordion>
	<Accordion title="Identify retention drop-off">
		Compare retention rate over the last 4 quarters. If it's dropping, investigate the receipt email, donor experience, and campaign relevance.
	</Accordion>
	<Accordion title="Validate acquisition campaigns">
		After a campaign, check New Donors. If new donors didn't move, the campaign reached existing donors only — fine for revenue, weak for growth.
	</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Privacy

Donor reports show personal data (names, emails). Access is gated by the same `manage_options` capability as the rest of DonorPress admin. For organizations with stricter privacy needs, a future release will add a "view-only" role that can see aggregates but not individual donor names.
