---
title: "Refunds"
description: "How to issue full and partial refunds in DonorPress, reconcile gateway fees, and keep donor receipts and reports accurate after refunds."
---

DonorPress supports both full and partial refunds for any gateway that exposes them (Stripe and PayPal both do).

## Full refunds

From a donation's detail screen:

<Steps>
	<Step title='Click "Refund"'>
		The button only appears if the donation status is **Complete** and the gateway supports refunds.
	</Step>
	<Step title="Confirm the amount">
		Defaults to the full original amount.
	</Step>
	<Step title="Add an optional reason">
		Stored in the activity log; sent to the gateway as refund metadata where supported.
	</Step>
	<Step title="Confirm">
		The refund is sent to the gateway. On success the donation status updates to **Refunded**.
	</Step>
</Steps>

Side effects:

- Donation status → **Refunded**.
- Donor's lifetime total recalculates (the refunded amount is excluded).
- A refund event is added to the activity log with the refund ID.
- A "Refund Issued" email is sent to the donor (configurable in **Settings → Email Notifications**).

## Partial refunds

Same flow, but enter a smaller amount. After a partial refund:

- Donation status remains **Complete**, with a `refunded_amount` field populated.
- The donor's lifetime total reflects only the net (original − refunded).
- You can issue additional partial refunds up to the original total.

## Reconciling refunds issued in the gateway

If you (or someone else) refund a donation directly in the Stripe or PayPal dashboard, DonorPress hears about it via the gateway webhook (`charge.refunded` for Stripe, `PAYMENT.CAPTURE.REFUNDED` for PayPal). The donation updates automatically — usually within seconds.

If webhooks aren't configured, you'll need to manually mark the donation refunded in DonorPress to keep your reports accurate.

## Time limits

Gateways have their own refund time limits:

- **Stripe**: 180 days from the original charge.
- **PayPal**: 180 days from the original capture (60 days for some account types).

After the limit, neither DonorPress nor the gateway dashboard can issue an automatic refund. You'd have to send the donor a manual reverse payment.

## Refund fees

Most gateways do not refund their processing fee when you issue a refund. So a $100 refund of a $100 donation typically costs you the original ~$3 fee. Plan accordingly.

## What if the refund fails?

Common reasons:

- Original charge was already refunded in the gateway.
- Past the gateway's refund time limit.
- Insufficient balance in your gateway account (rare; the gateway will pull from your bank if possible).

The gateway error message is shown in the DonorPress activity log so you can act on it.

## Subscriptions and refunds

Refunding a single recurring renewal does **not** cancel the subscription. To stop future renewals, cancel the subscription separately from the subscriptions admin.

## Receipt updates

When a donation is refunded, the donor's annual receipt PDF (if the [Annual Receipts module](/modules/annual-receipts) is on) automatically reflects the refund the next time it's downloaded — annual receipts are generated fresh each time, never cached.

## Bulk refunds

There's no bulk refund action. Refunds are intentionally one-at-a-time to prevent costly mistakes. If you need to mass-refund (e.g. an event was cancelled), use the gateway's batch refund tooling and let the webhooks update DonorPress.
